Due to extreme variation in elevation , great variation occurs in the climatic conditions of Himachal . The climate varies from hot and subhumid tropical in the southern tracts to , with more elevation , cold , alpine , and glacial in the northern and eastern mountain ranges . The state 's winter capital , Dharamsala receives very heavy rainfall , while areas like Lahaul and Spiti are cold and almost rainless . Broadly , Himachal experiences three seasons : summer , winter , and rainy season . Summer lasts from mid-April till the end of June and most parts become very hot ( except in the alpine zone which experiences a mild summer ) with the average temperature ranging from 28 to 32 ° C ( 82 to 90 ° F ) .
