By 00 : 15 UTC on September 6 , Irma reached peak intensity with 180 mph ( 285 km / h ) winds and a minimum pressure of 914 mbar ( 914 hPa ; 27.0 inHg ) . This ties it with Hurricane Mitch of 1998 and Hurricane Rita of 2005 as the fifth - strongest Atlantic hurricane by wind speed . Only four other Atlantic hurricanes have been recorded with wind speeds higher than Irma : Hurricane Allen of 1980 , which had maximum sustained winds of 190 mph ( 305 km / h ) , and the 1935 Labor Day hurricane , Hurricane Gilbert of 1988 , and Hurricane Wilma of 2005 , all of which had peak winds of 185 mph ( 295 km / h ) .
