The remainder of Act Two is set in the Proctor 's home . John and Elizabeth are incredulous that nearly forty people have been arrested for witchcraft based on the pronouncements of Abigail and the other girls . John knows their apparent possession and accusations of witchcraft are untrue , as Abigail told him when they were alone together in the first act , but is unsure of how to confess without revealing the affair . Elizabeth is disconcerted to learn her husband was alone with Abigail . She believes John still lusts after Abigail and tells him that as long as he does , he will never redeem himself .
