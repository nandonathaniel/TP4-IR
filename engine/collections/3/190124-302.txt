According to 2006 statistics , one in three people in the U.S. will contract an STI by the age of 24 and by the age of 20 , forty percent of women have been pregnant . According to the Center for Disease Control , young people ages 15 -- 24 account for 50 % of all new STIs , the most prevalent being HPV and chlamydia . Family planning in the United States can be expensive and often not covered by insurance plans . However , effective beginning September 23 , 2010 , following the passage of the Affordable Care Act , preventative services , including contraception , and STI screenings and counseling , are available to all insured women with no co-pay .
