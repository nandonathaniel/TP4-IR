The use of the word `` blood '' to refer to kin or familial relations has roots dating back to Greek and Roman traditions. . This usage of the term was common in the English - speaking world at least as early as the mid 1300s . Because English speakers around that time would have understood the word `` blood '' as referring to family , it is likely that the use of `` blood '' in the expression `` blood is thicker than water '' would also have been understood by English speakers referring to family .
