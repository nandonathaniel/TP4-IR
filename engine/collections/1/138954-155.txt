Latin spatha is derived from ancient Greek σπάθη . Therefore , the form spathula is more akin to its origin than spatula . Ancient Greek σπάθη has a similar meaning as Latin spatha , as any broad blade , and can also refer to a spatula or to the broad blade of a sword. , but also to the blade of an oar . The aforementioned πλάται for shoulder blades was also used for blades of an oar . Concordantly σπάθη was also used to refer to the shoulder blade .
