The largest species in the most species - rich passerine family , Tyrannidae or tyrant - flycatchers , is the great shrike - tyrant of the South Andes ( Agriornis lividus ) , at 99.2 g ( 3.50 oz ) and 31 cm ( 12 in ) , although the fork - tailed flycatcher ( Tyrannus savana ) , to 41 cm ( 16 in ) , is longer thanks to its extreme tail .
