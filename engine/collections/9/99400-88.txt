The imperative endings were presumably / - ∅ / , / - ī / and / - ū / for the second singular masculine , second singular feminine and second plural masculine respectively , but all three forms surface in the orthography as qtl , i.e. - ∅ . The old Semitic jussive , which originally differed slightly from the prefix conjugation , is no longer possible to separate from it in Phoenician with the present data .
