Trade between Spain and the Philippines was via the Pacific Ocean to Mexico ( Manila to Acapulco ) , and then across the Caribbean Sea and Atlantic Ocean to Spain ( Veracruz to CÃ¡diz ) . Manila became the most important center of trade in Asia between the 17th and 18th centuries . All sorts of products from China , Japan , Brunei , the Moluccas and even India were sent to Manila to be sold for silver 8 - Real coins which came aboard the galleons from Acapulco . These goods , including silk , porcelain , spices , lacquerware and textile products were then sent to Acapulco and from there to other parts of New Spain , Peru and Europe .
