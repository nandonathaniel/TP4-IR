Ireland has a common law legal system with a written constitution that provides for a parliamentary democracy . The court system consists of the Supreme Court , the Court of Appeal , the High Court , the Circuit Court and the District Court , all of which apply the law of Ireland . Trials for serious offences must usually be held before a jury . The High Court and the Supreme Court have authority , by means of judicial review , to determine the compatibility of laws and activities of other institutions of the state with the constitution and the law . Except in exceptional circumstances , court hearings must occur in public . The Criminal Courts of Justice is the principal building for the criminal courts . It includes the Dublin Metropolitan District Court , Court of Criminal Appeal , Dublin Circuit Criminal Court and Central Criminal Court .
