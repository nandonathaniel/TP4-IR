Baal ( / ˈbeɪəl / ) , properly Baʿal , meant `` owner '' and , by extension , `` lord '' , `` master '' , and `` husband '' in Hebrew and the other Northwest Semitic languages . In some early contexts and theophoric names , it and Baali ( / ˈbeɪəlaɪ / ; `` My Lord '' ) were treated as synonyms of Adon and Adonai . After the time of Solomon and particularly after Jezebel 's attempt to promote the worship of the Lord of Tyre Melqart , however , the name became particularly associated with the Canaanite storm god Baʿal Haddu and was gradually avoided as a title for Yahweh .
