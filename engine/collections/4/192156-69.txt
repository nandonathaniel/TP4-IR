Oghuz languages in Europe include Turkish , spoken in European Turkey and by immigrant communities ; Azerbaijani is spoken in parts of Southern Russia and Gagauz is spoken in Gagauzia . Kypchak languages in Europe include Crimean Tatar , which is spoken in Crimea ; Tatar , which is spoken in Tatarstan ; Bashkir , which is spoken in Bashkortostan ; and Kazakh , which is spoken in Kazakhstan . Oghur languages were historically indigenous to much of Eastern Europe ; however , most of them are extinct today , with the exception of Chuvash , which is spoken in Chuvashia .
