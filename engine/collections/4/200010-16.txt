The individual and society are indissoluble . Social constructionists question the Western idea of an autonomous individual who can draw a clear line between the self and the society . According to social constructionism , individuals can create meaning only in relation to what they are exposed to in their environment . Paradoxically , the same individuals co-create the meanings that are available in this environment . Marecek et al. conclude therefore that the society and the individual are indissoluble and mutually constitutive .
