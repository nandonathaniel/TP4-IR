Historian Tudor Parfitt has declared that `` the Lost Tribes are indeed nothing but a myth '' , and he writes that `` this myth is a vital feature of colonial discourse throughout the long period of European overseas empires , from the beginning of the fifteenth century , until the later half of the twentieth '' . Zvi Ben - Dor Benite states : `` The fascination with the tribes has generated , alongside ostensibly nonfictional scholarly studies , a massive body of fictional literature and folktale . '' Anthropologist Shalva Weil has documented differing tribes and peoples claiming affiliation to the Lost Tribes throughout the world .
