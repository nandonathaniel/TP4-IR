The cathedral was designed by Patrick Keely , a noted ecclesiastical architect , in the Gothic Revival style . Construction was supervised by John A. Dempwolf . The building , measuring 364 ft ( 111 m ) in length and 90 ft ( 27 m ) at the transepts , can accommodate about 2,000 worshippers in the main level . There is also an oratory for smaller services in the lower level . It is constructed of Roxbury puddingstone with gray limestone trim and reaches a height of 120 ft ( 37 m ) . A planned western spire was never completed .
