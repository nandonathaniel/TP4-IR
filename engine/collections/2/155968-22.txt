Also , DuPont executives marketing nylon as a revolutionary man - made material did not at first realize that some consumers experienced a sense of unease and distrust , even fear , towards synthetic fabrics . A particularly damaging news story , drawing on DuPont 's 1938 patent for the new polymer , suggested that one method of producing nylon might be to use cadaverine , a chemical extracted from corpses . Although scientists asserted that cadaverine was also extracted by heating coal , the public often refused to listen , as in the case of a woman who confronted one of the lead scientists at DuPont and refused to accept that the rumour was not true .
