After the war , Washington discovered that a much easier railroad route to California lay slightly south of the Gila River , in Mexico . In 1853 , President Santa Anna sold off the Gadsden Strip to the US for $10 million in the Gadsden Purchase . This loss of still more territory provoked considerable outrage among Mexicans , but Santa Anna claimed that he needed money to rebuild the army from the war . In the end , he kept or squandered most of it .
