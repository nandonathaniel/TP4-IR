Some African countries restrict or forbid dual citizenship . It is allowed for example in Angola , Burundi , Côte d'Ivoire , Djibouti , Gabon , Gambia , Ghana , Kenya , Mali , Morocco , Mozambique , Niger , Nigeria , Rwanda , Senegal , São Tomé and Príncipe , Sierra Leone , Sudan , Tunisia , Uganda and Zambia . Eritreans , Egyptians , and South Africans wanting to take another citizenship need a permission to maintain their citizenship . Eritrea taxes its citizens worldwide , even if they have never lived in the country . Lesotho restricts dual citizenship , but observes jus soli .
