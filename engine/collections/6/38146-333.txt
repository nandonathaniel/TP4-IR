On 20 June 1792 a number of armed women took part in a procession that `` passed through the halls of the Legislative Assembly , into the Tuileries Gardens , and then through the King 's residence . '' Militant women also assumed a special role in the funeral of Marat , following his murder on 13 July 1793 . As part of the funeral procession , they carried the bathtub in which Marat had been murdered ( by a counter-revolutionary woman ) as well as a shirt stained with Marat 's blood . On 20 May 1793 women were at the fore of a crowd that demanded `` bread and the Constitution of 1793 . '' When their cries went unnoticed , the women went on a rampage , `` sacking shops , seizing grain and kidnapping officials . ''
